FUN FACTS

  1. F1 racers lose over 3 litres of body water during 1 race

    According to Telegraph, the average F1 is equipped with 1.5 liters of water in the car, which is hooked to the helmet. This, in most cases, isn't enough to keep the drivers hydrated for long enough. It thus takes a lot of endurance and physical fitness to be a Formula race car driver.

  2. Car tires lose 0.5kgs during a race

    Formula 1 car tires lose 0.5 kgs during the race. This is because of the wear and tear that comes as a result of the high speed and the abrupt breaking. The G-Force doesn't spare the tires.

    Itâ€™s not just tires, but also racers who lose weight during an F1 race. The main reason why Formula 1 drivers lose up to 4 KGs is the unbearable temperatures in the cockpit, which can reach up to 50 degrees Celsius.

  4. The F1 Helmets are the toughest in the world

    F1 helmets should be extremely light. This presents the challenge of coming up with a product that can also be as tough as it needs to be. To ensure that the helmets meet the strict requirements, they have to go through a couple of fragmentation and deformation tests. Carbon fiber is the main material that's used to make F1 helmets because of its robustness.

  5. Juan Fangio is the oldest racer to win an F1 title

    The oldest driver to win a title was Juan Manuel Fangio (the elder), who collected the final of his five world titles in 1957 at the age of 47. The oldest race winner, Luigi Fagioli, who won the 1951 French Grand Prix for Alfa Romeo, aged 53 years and 22 days.

  6. Formula 1 cars have 25 buttons on the steering wheel

    If you think your modern steering wheel is complicated, the Formula 1 drivers have it worse. Their steering wheels can have upwards of 25 buttons on them. Each button and switch controls every aspect of the car, allowing the driver ultimate control.

  7. F1 cars accelerate from 0 to 100 MPH and hit back to 0 within 4 seconds

    Imagine accelerating to 100 mph and returning to zero in just 4 seconds. You might not survive if you have a heart condition. The effect is like feeling your body being squeezed with an invisible force. Breath control is essential in such a situation, and F1 drivers have to go through several drills to prepare for G-Force.
